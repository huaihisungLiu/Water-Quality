# Poop Patrol 💧
### A water-pollution awareness project for kids

**Poop Patrol** is a product-discovery project. The goal is a web app that helps
elementary-school kids — specifically 5th graders — understand *and actually care
about* water pollution, instead of tuning out the reading-heavy, textbook-style
materials they usually get.

The name leans into the hook: the sewage side of the Tijuana River story. It is meant
to be funny and memorable — a name a 10-year-old will repeat.

This site documents how the idea is being developed. Rather than jumping straight to
building, the project follows the **UA Framework** — a staged discovery process that
pushes every belief to be defensible with evidence before moving forward.

---

## What we're making

| | |
|---|---|
| **Product** | Poop Patrol — a water-pollution awareness web app |
| **Who it's for** | 5th-grade students (~10–11), reached through their science teacher |
| **The problem** | Kids find textbook-style pollution material boring, so it doesn't stick |
| **The angle** | Make it funny, memorable, and screen-native instead of dry reading |

---

## Where the project is right now

The project is in **Stage 1 — Discovery**, working on the **first-draft positioning
statement**. Three of the seven positioning clauses have a first belief written down;
the rest are still being worked out. No user research or interviews have happened yet —
that's what comes next, and it's how the current beliefs get tested.
