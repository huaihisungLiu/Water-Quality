# The Process

Poop Patrol isn't being built by guessing. It's being developed with the
**UA Framework** — a staged product-discovery process. The whole idea is to take a
hunch ("kids should care more about water pollution") and slowly turn each part of it
into something you can defend with evidence, *before* spending time building the wrong
thing.

## How it works

The framework moves through stages. Each stage has a clear deliverable, and you don't
race ahead — you revisit and sharpen your thinking in passes as you learn more.

1. **Stage 1 — Discovery.** Figure out who the product is really for and what problem
   it solves. The deliverable is a **positioning statement**: a single, tight paragraph
   naming the customer, the need, the product, its category, its key benefit, the main
   alternative, and how it's different. Every clause starts as a belief and has to earn
   its place through **research** and **interviews**.

2. **Design & prototyping.** Once the positioning is grounded, turn it into something
   you can see and react to — starting with **wireframes** of the actual experience.

Each step below has its own page with a short explanation of *what the step is* and a

## The honest part

Right now the project is early. The positioning statement is written from **belief**,
not yet from evidence — and that's on purpose. The framework says: write down what you
think first, be explicit about *why* you think it, then go find out whether you're
right. The pages below show exactly where each belief stands.
