# Research

**Status: ⚪ Upcoming — not started yet**

## What this step is

Research is where the beliefs in the positioning statement meet reality. Instead of
asking real people right away, you first find out what's already knowable: What does the
5th-grade science curriculum actually cover? What water-pollution materials already exist
for kids? Who else is trying to solve this, and how? The point is to sharpen — or throw
out — beliefs before spending anyone's interview time on them.

## The results so far

No research has been done yet. The project is still at the first-draft positioning stage,
so this page will fill in as work happens.

### What research needs to answer

These are the open questions the current beliefs depend on:

- **Curriculum fit.** Is water pollution genuinely part of the 5th-grade science
  curriculum, or is the natural age older or younger?
- **The real bottleneck.** Is *engagement* actually the problem, or is it class time and
  curriculum standards? Do existing materials already engage kids well?
- **The landscape.** What water-pollution learning tools already exist for this age group,
  and where do they fall short?
- **The name.** Would "Poop Patrol" clear a school content filter, or is a tamer name
  needed?

When research produces answers, this page will summarize what was found and which
positioning clauses moved from *belief* toward *evidence*.
