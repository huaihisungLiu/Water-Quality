# Interviews

**Status: ⚪ Upcoming — not started yet**

## What this step is

Interviews are where you talk to the actual people in the positioning statement — here,
that means **science teachers** (the gatekeepers who would assign the app) and **5th
graders** (the kids who would use it). The goal isn't to pitch the idea; it's to listen
for whether the problem is real, whether it matters to them, and whether the assumptions
behind Poop Patrol hold up.

## The results so far

No interviews have happened yet. This is one of the project's known gaps — the idea is
currently a hunch, with no conversations behind it. This page will fill in once
interviews begin.

### Who to talk to, and what to learn

- **Science teachers** — Is water pollution something you teach? Would you ever assign an
  outside website? What makes a resource worth your limited class time? Would the name
  "Poop Patrol" be a problem?
- **5th graders** — How do you feel about the pollution material you get now? What makes a
  learning site fun instead of boring? Does the "gross-out" angle actually land?

### What this step will confirm or change

The teacher conversations especially could reshape the whole positioning statement — for
example, if teachers say pollution isn't in their curriculum, or that they'd never assign
an outside site. Interview findings will be summarized here and folded back into the
**[Positioning Statement]({{ '/positioning/' | relative_url }})**.
